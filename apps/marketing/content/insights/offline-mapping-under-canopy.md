## The field reality

Under dense canopy, smartphone GNSS accuracy often drops to 5–15 meters. Connectivity disappears without warning. Compliance tools designed for office Wi‑Fi fail the people who actually map plots.

## Waypoint averaging instead of hard lockouts

Tracebud records a stream of coordinates at each vertex and calculates a mean position over 60–120 seconds. This filters multipath interference instead of rejecting captures with a rigid HDOP threshold.

## Offline fail-safe

When GNSS fails entirely, teams can trace perimeters against pre-cached satellite imagery on the device. Data syncs when connectivity returns — with conflict ordering that does not trust client wall clocks alone.

## What this means for the chain

Origin data captured reliably at the farm becomes reusable evidence for cooperatives, exporters, and importers. One capture event feeds the whole network.
